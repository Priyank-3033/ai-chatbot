# Customer Support Knowledge Base

## Account Access
Customers can reset their password from the login page by clicking "Forgot Password".
Password reset links expire after 30 minutes for security reasons.
If a customer no longer has access to their email inbox, a support agent must manually verify identity before updating the email address.

## Billing and Refunds
Monthly subscriptions renew automatically at the start of each billing cycle.
Customers can download invoices from the Billing section of the dashboard.
Refund requests are eligible within 14 days of the last charge for annual plans, provided usage is below the fair-use threshold.
If a customer was charged twice, support should verify the invoice history and payment references before promising a refund.
If the refund request falls outside policy, the chatbot should explain the policy clearly and route the case to a human agent instead of making an exception.

## Shipping and Orders
Standard shipping takes 3 to 5 business days.
Expedited shipping takes 1 to 2 business days.
Customers receive a tracking link by email once the order has been packed.
Orders can only be modified before they reach the packed state.
If the order is still in the placed state, customers can request a shipping address update.
If the order is already packed, shipped, or out for delivery, the chatbot should explain that address changes are no longer guaranteed and suggest contacting support for the best available option.

## Returns and Damaged Items
Customers should report damaged, defective, or missing items as soon as possible after delivery.
Support should ask for the order number, a short issue summary, and clear photos when the item arrives damaged.
If the item is confirmed damaged in transit, the case should move to replacement or refund handling based on customer preference and stock availability.
The chatbot should not promise replacement stock unless inventory is confirmed.

## Troubleshooting Login Issues
If a customer sees repeated "invalid credentials" errors, first confirm caps lock is off and the correct workspace is selected.
If multi-factor authentication fails, ask the customer to resync their authenticator app or request backup codes.
If the issue persists, collect the timestamp, browser, and screenshot before escalating.

## Payment Failures
If a payment attempt fails, customers should first confirm the amount was not already captured before retrying.
For pending payments, advise the customer to wait a few minutes and refresh the order history before creating a duplicate order.
If money was debited but the order was not created, the chatbot should collect the payment reference and route the case for payment reconciliation.

## Account and Security
The chatbot can help with password reset, login access, billing questions, order tracking, and shipping guidance.
The chatbot should not verify identity documents, change account ownership, or unlock disputed accounts by itself.
For suspicious login alerts, compromised accounts, or ownership conflicts, the case must be escalated to a human support agent.

## Escalation Policy
Escalate to a human agent when a question involves legal disputes, security incidents, refunds outside policy, or account ownership conflicts.
The chatbot should never invent policy exceptions.
Escalate when confidence is low, when customer sentiment is severe, or when the requested action would change money movement, account access, or ownership.
